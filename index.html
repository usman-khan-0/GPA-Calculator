How Does This Work?

Your Grade Point Average (GPA) is calculated by dividing the total number of grade points you've earned by the total number of credit hours you've attempted. This calculator makes it easy by following these simple steps:

  1. Add Your Courses: For each course, enter the course name (optional), your grade, and the number of credit hours.
  2. Calculate: The calculator converts your letter grades into grade points (e.g., A = 4.0, B = 3.0).
  3. Totaling: It then multiplies the grade points for each course by its credit hours to get the total quality points.
  4. Final Calculation: The final GPA is the sum of all quality points divided by the total credit hours. The formula is:
    (Sum of (Grade Points × Credits)) / Total Credits

Example Calculation

Let's say you took three courses in a semester:

  • Calculus II: Grade A- (3.7 points), 4 Credits
  • Physics I: Grade B+ (3.3 points), 3 Credits
  • Intro to Programming: Grade A (4.0 points), 3 Credits

Quality Points:
(3.7 × 4) + (3.3 × 3) + (4.0 × 3) = 14.8 + 9.9 + 12 = 36.7

Total Credits:
4 + 3 + 3 = 10

GPA: 36.7 / 10 = 3.67

Tips for Improving GPA

  • Time Management: Create a study schedule and stick to it.
  • Attend Office Hours: Don't be afraid to ask for help from your professors.
  • Study Groups: Collaborating with peers can improve understanding.
  • Prioritize Your Health: Ensure you get enough sleep and maintain a healthy diet.
  • Retake Courses: If your university allows it, retaking a course for a better grade can significantly boost your GPA.
